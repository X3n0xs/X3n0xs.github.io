02 — Bio-City

Restoration of the Cultural & Natural Connection in the City

Location

Bio-city is a project that attempts to solve the problem of dense housing in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. In exchange for the development project, a lake (Beoung Kok) was filled to prepare for new high-rise building.

"Bio" refers to the life including the life of the natural landscape, and the life of the people who live as part of nature as well. While "City" refers to the habitat of the human being.

The memories of nature — the lake and the forest — and the identity of the people, lifestyle, and culture, especially Cambodians where water was once cherished and culture was built around water, have been lost and blurred with modernity.

Site area: 24,500 sqm.

Conceptual Form

Bio-city firstly respects the elements of nature that once existed in the site and re-imagines them. The feeling of the lake is re-created along its axis; dug-up soil is refilled as hills, creating a natural landscape.

The chaotic arrangement from unplanned development at the north is respected, extending from surrounding shop houses inwards. Recreation spaces are arranged in the hills and lakes naturally while residential spaces are kept free from the ground level — enhancing the sensation of living in a traditional village that once existed in the lake.

Floor Plans

Shops, café and food stalls activate the ground floor. The building consists of a core in the middle of four units — three studios and a family unit — arranged for ventilation from the prominent north wind while keeping east and west sides shaded.

Family Unit

The family unit embraces cultivating green vegetation for natural ventilation, sunlight, and immersion with nature at home. Water elements, green spaces, wood and hammock recall traditional houses.

Renderings

Green corridor: activating the ground level with vendors and activities while upper floors remain residential. Interiors invite natural elements, recreate family relationships in a traditional lifestyle, and unify vertical farming with Khmer culture.

Facade Details

The facade system allows each panel to be stationary, slid, or pivoted to maintain vegetation. Variation of facade panels creates a constantly changing elevation along the public edge.
