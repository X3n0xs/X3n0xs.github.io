03 — Other experiments

Picasso’s Viewing Gallery

Picasso's Viewing Gallery is an experiment of form following the concept of cubism. It takes three paintings of The Accordionist and divides them from different perceptions of the accordionist.

The material is adapted to show the difference of elements — solid, reflective, transparent, and semi-transparent. The structure is adapted to each material as solid load-bearing structure, frame, and tensile structure.

The result is a statement of strong element in the center, tilted outwards, supported by light elements. Walking through the space, one experiences changes of light, shadow, reflection, and solidity as material properties differ.
