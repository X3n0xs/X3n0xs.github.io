3D Urban Segmentation from Street-View Imagery

Reconstructing segmented point-cloud environments from panoramic street-view sequences so urban scenes can be analyzed in 3D—not only as flattened 360° views.

Leng Monykandy, Zongchao Gu, Jun Cao · Southeast University

Problem

Street-view imagery is widely used in urban analysis, but most workflows treat the city as a set of viewpoints on the street. That flattens 3D spatial relations and makes it harder to reuse the same capture for deeper tasks such as risk assessment, simulation, or mid-to-small scale urban studies. Building and maintaining full city models traditionally also demands heavy resources and continuous updates.

Approach

This work reconstructs 3D spatial structure from panoramic images collected along a road corridor, then combines the resulting point cloud with image segmentation to produce a segmented 3D environment. The emphasis is on using readily available street-level panoramic data to recover both geometry and semantic labels in one representation.

Detailed pipeline parameters and implementation code are not published here; the public materials focus on the research framing, outputs, and applications.

Findings

The method yields a point-cloud scene that carries both spatial relations and segmentation information. That dual output can support urban applications beyond visual inspection, including seismic risk / safe-zone exploration, solar-exposure studies from new viewpoints, and pathways toward mesh-based simulations such as thermal comfort analysis.

A compact corridor case study (Koh Pich / Diamond Island, Phnom Penh) was used to demonstrate reconstruction and segmentation under dense roadside building conditions.

Contribution

Shows how panoramic street-view sequences can be turned into segmented 3D urban scenes that preserve spatial relations for analysis—enabling mid-to-small scale urban studies without relying on continuously maintained, resource-heavy city models.
