Facade Debris Risk Zones from Street-Level Imagery

Assessing building-facade vulnerability and pedestrian debris-fall risk zones during seismic events using street-view imagery—without requiring heavy on-site survey campaigns.

Leng Monykandy, Zongchao Gu · Southeast University

Problem

In seismically active cities, identifying where facade failure could endanger people on the street is important for resilience planning. Conventional hazard evaluation often depends on extensive fieldwork and specialized instruments, which limits how widely such assessments can be applied across an urban network.

Approach

This research uses street-view / street-level photographic sequences to reconstruct scene geometry, segment the 3D point cloud, extract building layout cues, and estimate debris-fall and safer pedestrian zones on the street. The goal is a practical, image-based reading of risk and safe circulation space for planning and emergency preparedness.

Implementation details and full processing scripts are kept offline; this page summarizes the research question, outputs, and contribution.

Findings

Key outputs demonstrated in the conference materials include:

  • segmented 3D point clouds derived from 360° / street-level imagery
  • building layout extraction from the reconstructed scene
  • estimation of debris-fall areas and safer street zones for pedestrians during seismic events

These outputs are intended to support evacuation thinking, priority areas for structural attention, and more accessible hazard mapping from existing urban imagery.

Contribution

Provides an accessible street-imagery pathway to map facade-related debris risk and safer pedestrian zones—helping planners move from disaster-resilience goals toward concrete street-level hazard reading without relying only on costly field campaigns.
